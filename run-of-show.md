### Part 3: The Framework (15 min)

**The million dollar bag:**

"Some of you might be feeling like this is an exercise in how to approach anybody in public. Like, how do you have confidence going up to a total stranger and asking for money?"

"Imagine you're single. There's an attractive person sitting at a bar. You're trying to amp yourself up to go say hi. Now imagine I'm sitting next to you and I hand you a bag with a million dollars in it. Go give this person the bag."

"You'd walk right over. If they were on the phone, you'd say 'can you please hang up, I have something really important to tell you.' They'd hang up. You'd hand them the bag. They'd be delighted."

"This is our starting point. The core of every outbound motion is you need to hand somebody a million dollars. A promise worth keeping. A promise worth people stopping what they're doing, giving you their attention, and more importantly, their money."

"Waste less metal by this afternoon. That's a million dollar bag. You should want that."

**The list is the message:**

"Our first exercise is asset generation. I'd like you to have a list of people to reach out to. But not just any list."

"The reason why we select specific people is the very reason why we're reaching out. The list IS the message."

"Think about 248. We went from 'all software sales teams' to 'field sales teams that sell to restaurants and bars.' That specificity made the outreach write itself. It made us easier to win."

**The Who / Why / Where / When framework:**

| Element | What it means | Example (Offline) |
|---------|--------------|-------------------|
| **Who** | A specific group of people you can help | Run club organizers who want to do this full time but can't quit their day job |
| **Why** | The exact way you're helping them. Built into the targeting. | "You're growing your run club but also work full time. What if brands paid you?" |
| **Where** | How you find them | SerpAPI searches, Instagram, LinkedIn verification |
| **When** | The trigger. When do you reach out? | When they hit 10K followers but still have a full-time job on LinkedIn |

"When your targeting is specific enough that the outreach copy writes itself, you've found the right niche."

**Building the muscle of go-to-market predictability:**

"You need to build a muscle of go-to-market predictability. This is a thing you need to get good at as a company. And software engineering helps because you literally save your progress. Your agent remembers everything. The list, the emails, the follow-ups. You build on top of what you built before."

### Part 4: Company Deep Dives (60 min)

**For each company (7-8 min each):**
1. Share the ICP we drafted (from the repo)
2. Ask 2-3 questions from the workshop questions doc
3. Show the asset we already built (lead count, data)
4. Identify where they are on the readiness ladder

**Order and key moments:**

#### LineSight (8 min)
- **Show:** 72,367 metal businesses filtered from 12M Google Maps records. 32K core.
- **Ask:** "Elite Steel is your one customer. What does Elite Steel do every day that makes LineSight essential? If I gave you 72K leads, how would you filter to the 500 that respond tomorrow?"
- **Reveal:** The killer filter is scraping websites for "slitting" and "coil." That cuts 72K to maybe 5K.

#### CloudReno (8 min)
- **Show:** 87,255 K&B remodelers. 42K core.
- **Ask:** "What does a founding partner look like that the other 23,997 don't? Be brutally specific."
- **Reveal:** The qualifying signal is on LinkedIn. Owner age 55+, no visible #2, 15+ years in business. The outreach writes itself: "You've built this for 22 years. What's your exit plan?"

#### Seated with Love (7 min)
- **Show:** 164,715 venue leads. 39K core wedding venues.
- **Ask:** "At $14.99/month, why isn't every venue signing up? What's the real blocker?"
- **Reveal:** Cross-reference with The Knot reviews. 50+ reviews = 30+ weddings/year. Scrape for "preferred vendor list" page. That's the monetization hook.

#### WattShift (7 min)
- **Show:** 188,737 leads. 6K core solar, 1,669 in CA/NY priority markets.
- **Ask:** "Four partner segments is three too many. Which one converts fastest?"
- **Reveal:** Solar installers in NEM 3.0 markets whose website does NOT mention batteries. The absence is the signal.

#### Offline (8 min)
- **Show:** 174 run clubs across 15 cities, ranked by Instagram followers. @chicagoruncollective (39K), @phx_run (24K), etc.
- **Ask:** "If you could only work with one type of community in one city for 6 months, what would it be?"
- **Reveal:** The organizer's LinkedIn profile is the qualifying data. Full-time job + growing community = "you want to do this full time but can't quit." The list is the message.

#### Plexus (7 min)
- **Show:** Google Maps isn't the right source. 23K low-quality leads.
- **Ask:** "If I put you in a room with 10 AV engineers, 10 satellite operators, and 10 robotics founders, which group leans forward?"
- **Reveal:** The right data source is Crunchbase + LinkedIn + GitHub. Demo finding 10 AV companies and the hardware eng lead at each.

#### Kaizen Design (7 min)
- **Show:** 580 health system domains with 10+ facilities. Top: VA (1,273), Ascension (237), Cleveland Clinic (211).
- **Ask:** "Can you name 10 health systems with this problem right now? Not hospitals. Health systems."
- **Reveal:** This is a 50-person target list, not a 50,000-person list. Account-based, not volume-based.

#### ForgeBee (8 min)
- **Show:** 831 bee/honey operations, 3,795 CA Central Valley farms.
- **Ask:** "How many QMCs have you sold? To whom? That's your current business. Everything else is vision."
- **Reveal:** Near-term list is 30 university bee research labs. Search PubMed, find the PI, send them a demo video.

## BLOCK 2 (2 hours): Live Demos + Hands-On

### Part 5: The GTM Stack Demo (20 min)

**"Let me show you how I actually do this."**

Live demo of the full pipeline on one lead. Pick a company from the room (ideally LineSight or CloudReno, cleanest data).

1. **Find** - Pull a lead from the list we already built
2. **Qualify** - FireCrawl scrapes their website. Agent reads it and confirms the ICP match.
3. **Enrich** - Apollo people search finds the buyer's name and title
4. **Verify** - Email waterfall (Kitt, Findymail, LeadMagic, Prospeo + NeverBounce) finds and verifies the email
5. **Write** - Claude generates the cold email. Spam checker confirms clean. No em dashes, no buzzwords, no fluff.
6. **Send** - Ready for SmartLead or send directly from inbox

**The gtmcanon moment:** "My agent doesn't know which tool to use. It asks gtmcanon.com: 'I need to find this person's email.' And it gets back the right API call with a working example. That's the whole point. You don't need to memorize 20 APIs. The agent discovers the right tool for every step."

**Show the repo:** "Everything I just did is in the repo. The scripts, the API patterns, the framework. Clone it tonight."

### Part 6: Company-by-Company Live Exercises (80 min)

**Split into two groups based on readiness:**

**Group A: List-build teams (40 min working, then share)**
- LineSight, CloudReno, Seated with Love, WattShift, Offline
- Exercise: Take your filtered list. Qualify 5 leads live. For each: scrape the website, find the buyer on LinkedIn, write the cold email. If the email writes itself, your ICP is right.

**Group B: Named-account teams (40 min working, then share)**
- Plexus, Kaizen Design, ForgeBee
- Exercise: Build your "first 10" list. 10 named humans with name, title, company, LinkedIn URL, and one sentence about why this person specifically would care. Then write 3 cold emails.

**Mert floats between groups, giving feedback and pushing teams to be more specific.**

**Key coaching moments to look for:**
- Team struggling to write the email? ICP needs more work. Push them to add another qualifying filter.
- Team writes email in 30 seconds? They've found their niche. Push them to send it right now.
- Team arguing about which segment to pick? Force a decision. "Pick one. You can always change it next week. But you can't test two at once."
